    • About Albus

    Albus Dumbledore was an English half-blood wizard who was born in August 1881 and died on June 30, 1997. He was a professor of Defence Against the Dark Arts and later Transfiguration at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. He also served as Headmaster of Hogwarts, Supreme Mugwump of the International Confederation of Wizards, and Chief Warlock of the Wizengamot. Dumbledore was considered to have been one of the greatest wizards of modern times.

    Dumbledore was known for his intelligence, kindness, and wisdom. He had a keen mind and was a legendary figure in the wizarding world. He was also known for his work on alchemy with Nicolas Flamel and his discovery of the twelve uses of dragon’s blood.

    Dumbledore founded and led the Order of the Phoenix during the First Wizarding War. He also played a crucial role in defeating Lord Voldemort during the Second Wizarding War. Dumbledore was laid to rest at Hogwarts and a portrait of him still remains at the school.

    Dumbledore enjoyed listening to chamber music and ten-pin bowling. He also had a fondness for sweets, particularly sherbet lemons.
